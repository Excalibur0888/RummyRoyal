Our Commitment to Responsible Gaming

At RummyRoyal, we are committed to providing a safe, secure, and responsible gaming environment. We believe that rummy should be played for entertainment and enjoyment, not as a way to make money or escape from problems.

We encourage all our players to play responsibly and within their means. This page provides information and resources to help you maintain control over your gaming habits.

Signs of Problem Gaming

It's important to recognize the signs of problem gaming. If you experience any of the following, it may be time to reassess your gaming habits:

  • Spending more time or money on gaming than you can afford
  • Neglecting work, education, or family responsibilities due to gaming
  • Gaming to escape from problems or relieve feelings of helplessness, guilt, anxiety, or depression
  • Lying to family members or others about how much you play
  • Feeling irritable or restless when trying to cut down on gaming
  • Chasing losses or increasing bets to try to win back money
  • Borrowing money or selling possessions to fund gaming
  • Continuing to play despite negative consequences

Tips for Responsible Gaming

Set Time Limits

Decide how much time you want to spend playing and stick to it. Use alarms or timers to help you keep track.

Set Budget Limits

Only play with money you can afford to lose. Set a budget and never exceed it.

Play for Entertainment

Remember that gaming should be for fun, not a way to make money or solve financial problems.

Stay Alert

Don't play when you're tired, stressed, or under the influence of alcohol or drugs.

Balance Your Life

Make sure gaming doesn't interfere with your work, education, relationships, or other responsibilities.

Take Breaks

Regular breaks help maintain perspective and prevent gaming from becoming compulsive.

Self-Assessment

Ask yourself these questions to assess your gaming habits:

  1. Do you spend more time or money on gaming than you intended?
  2. Have you ever lied to loved ones about how much you play?
  3. Do you play to escape problems or relieve negative feelings?
  4. Have you tried to cut back on gaming but couldn't?
  5. Do you feel restless or irritable when not gaming?
  6. Has gaming negatively affected your relationships, job, or education?
  7. Do you chase losses by playing more to win back money?
  8. Have you borrowed money or sold possessions to fund gaming?

If you answered "yes" to several of these questions, you may want to consider seeking help.

Resources for Help

If you or someone you know is struggling with gaming addiction, help is available. Here are some resources:

Gambling Therapy

Free online support and counseling for gambling problems.

GamCare

Information, advice, and support for anyone affected by gambling problems.

www.gamcare.org.uk

Gamblers Anonymous

A fellowship of men and women who share their experience, strength, and hope to solve their common problem.

Confidential support for those affected by gambling problems in India.

1800-11-0031
